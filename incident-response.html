The Incident Response domain ensures that the organization is prepared to detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents impacting the Cardholder Data Environment (CDE). The evidence below is consolidated for functional stakeholders (e.g., security, IT, compliance teams), using practical, real-world artifacts aligned with PCI DSS v4.0.1 requirements.

Evidence Requirement PCI DSS Requirement(s) Description and Practical Examples
Incident Response Plan 12.10.1, 12.10.2 A documented plan outlining procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents affecting the CDE, including roles, responsibilities, and contact information for stakeholders (e.g., legal, communications). Example: A PDF document titled "Incident Response Plan" detailing steps for identifying a CDE breach, escalation to the security team, and notification to legal and payment brands, with a contact list including CISO and external counsel.
Incident Response Training Records 12.10.3 Records of annual training for personnel with incident response responsibilities, covering their roles and procedures in the plan. Example: A training completion report from a platform (e.g., KnowBe4) listing security team members who completed incident response training, with signed acknowledgments in PDF.
Incident Response Testing Records 12.10.4 Records of annual testing of the incident response plan (e.g., tabletop exercises or simulations), including results, identified issues, and remediation actions. Example: A PDF report summarizing a tabletop exercise simulating a CDE data breach, documenting participant actions, identified gaps (e.g., delayed escalation), and corrective measures implemented.
Incident Response Monitoring Processes 12.10.5 Documented processes for 24/7 monitoring and detection of security incidents in the CDE, with procedures for immediate response. Example: An SOP in Word outlining 24/7 SIEM monitoring (e.g., Splunk) for CDE incidents, with sample SIEM alert emails showing real-time detection of unauthorized access attempts.
Incident Response Logs 12.10.6 Logs or records of actual security incidents or alerts in the CDE, documenting response actions, investigations, and resolutions. Example: A ServiceNow incident ticket detailing a detected CDE server compromise, including investigation steps (e.g., log analysis), containment actions (e.g., server isolation), and resolution (e.g., patched vulnerability).
Business Continuity Integration (DESV) A3.3.3 For Designated Entities, documentation showing integration of incident response with business continuity and disaster recovery plans, ensuring CDE protection during disruptions. Example: A PDF section in the incident response plan linking to the business continuity plan, detailing CDE system recovery procedures (e.g., restoring payment servers from backups), with test results from a recovery drill.
